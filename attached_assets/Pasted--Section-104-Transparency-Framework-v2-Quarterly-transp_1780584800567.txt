**Section 107 — Platform Moat**
```
Six defensible moats:

1. Data Moat
Quality interaction data trains better recommendations
over time. Competitors cannot replicate years of
quality-signal data.

2. Community Moat
Thoughtful communities are hard to migrate.
Network effects grow with quality contributor density.

3. Reputation Moat
User reputation scores built over years.
Leaving means starting over.

4. Creator Moat
Top creators have audience and analytics history.
Switching cost is high.

5. Trust Moat
Anonymity + transparency builds unique platform trust.
Hard to replicate without same architectural decisions.

6. Algorithm Moat
Quality Score algorithm improves with scale.
Early mover advantage in quality-signal training data.
```

**Section 110 — Founder Operating Principles**
```
These ten principles govern every future decision.
They are immutable unless changed by unanimous
founding team agreement.

Principle 1: Quality is the product.
Every metric, every feature, every decision must
serve quality discussion. If it doesn't, don't build it.

Principle 2: Anonymity is sacred.
Never compromise the anonymity promise.
It is the foundation of honest participation.

Principle 3: Trust is earned slowly and lost instantly.
Every safety, privacy, and moderation decision
must be made as if the platform's survival depends on it.

Principle 4: Creators are partners, not content farms.
Build every creator tool as if you are building
for someone whose livelihood depends on this platform.

Principle 5: Speed serves thought.
The platform must be fast enough that it never
interrupts the thinking process.

Principle 6: Transparency is not optional.
Publish everything that can be published.
Users who understand the platform trust it more.

Principle 7: Safety before growth.
Never sacrifice user safety for growth metrics.
A safe platform grows. An unsafe platform collapses.

Principle 8: Simplicity is discipline.
Every feature added removes something from focus.
Build less. Build better.

Principle 9: The algorithm serves the user.
The recommendation engine exists to serve reader curiosity,
not to maximize session time at the expense of wellbeing.

Principle 10: Build for the long term.
Decisions that sacrifice long-term trust for
short-term metrics are always wrong.
```
